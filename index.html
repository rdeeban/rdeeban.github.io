Bio

I am currently an undergraduate senior at Cornell University (Class of 2018) pursuing a degree in B.S. Computer Science and minoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering and pure Mathematics. I am a McMullen Cornell Engineering Dean Scholar, Meinig Family Cornell National Scholar, and Thomas Dinwoodie McMullen Scholar.

During the Summer of 2017, I worked as a software engineering intern at Microsoft Azure RnD. I designed and implemented a proof-of-concept for Multi-tenant Container-based Layer 7 Load Balancing as a Service. I presented optimality of project to Azure Networking.

I was an undergraduate researcher for Dr. John Hopcroft in Deep Learning at Cornell University. We designed a novel algorithm to traverse vectors between distributions in convolutional feature space.

During the Summer of 2016, I worked as a software engineering intern at NVIDIA. I developed tooling to debug Nvidia Grid Network cluster. I developed pipeline for deployment of storage service in cluster.

During the Summer of 2015, I worked as a Software Engineering Intern at Hewlett-Packard Inc. I developed a plugin feature for the HP JetAdvantage Printer Fleet Management.

During the Spring of 2015, I worked with Dr. Graeme Bailey on a Masters of Engineering Research Project at Cornell University. I designed a real-time subscribe/broadcast event-driven communication framework.

During the Summer of 2014, I worked as a Software Engineering Intern at White Cloud Analytics.

February 2012 to June 2014, I was an Intern Software Developer at Idaho Digital Learning Academy. I presented first web application at the age of 16 to the Idaho Senate Education Committee and received a Letter of Recommendation from one of the Senators.

Research Interests: Machine Learning (emphasis on Deep Learning applications in the domains of Computer Vision, Audio Recognition, and Cloud Computing), Networked Distributed Systems, Signals and Systems Analysis
